The Bohuslän coast, north of Gothenburg on Sweden's west coast, is one of the finest granite climbing areas in Europe. Its rolling pink and grey granite, rising from forest and sea, forms immaculate slabs, clean cracks, and steep walls, with thousands of routes spread across a vast and beautiful coastal landscape. Bohuslän is the heart of Swedish climbing, a place where the rock, the sea, and the long summer days combine in a destination of rare quality. Find it on the [map](/map?country=se).

## The Setting

Bohuslän is a coastal region of granite, forest, and sea, its rounded rock outcrops rising among pines and overlooking the Skagerrak. The crags are scattered across the landscape, from roadside to remote, often with views over the water and the archipelago. The setting is quintessentially Scandinavian — clean granite, dark forest, and the cool light of the northern summer — and the combination of quality rock and beautiful coastal surroundings is at the heart of Bohuslän's appeal.

## The Rock

The granite of Bohuslän is superb: a rolling, pink-and-grey rock that forms immaculate friction slabs, clean cracks, and steep walls. The slab climbing is famous, demanding delicate footwork and trust in friction on the smooth, grippy rock, while the cracks offer excellent trad climbing on natural protection. The rock quality is high throughout, and the variety of styles — from technical slabs to powerful cracks and faces — gives Bohuslän a deep and rewarding range of climbing.

## Slab Climbing and Trad

Bohuslän is celebrated for its slab climbing, on the rounded granite that gives some of the finest friction slabs in Europe. This delicate, balance-based climbing rewards precise footwork and a calm head. The crag also holds excellent crack and face climbing, much of it traditionally protected, and the strong Swedish ethic of clean climbing runs through the area. Together, the slabs and cracks make Bohuslän a complete granite destination for the trad and slab climber.

## The Crags and Sectors

Bohuslän holds thousands of routes spread across a vast number of crags throughout the region. The density and spread mean a climber can explore for a long time, following the rock and the conditions across the coastal landscape. Areas range from popular roadside crags to quieter, more remote venues, each with its own character. The sheer breadth of quality climbing is a major part of what makes Bohuslän one of Scandinavia's great destinations.

## Season and the Summer Light

Bohuslän is a summer destination. The granite comes into condition once the cold has passed, and the long days of the Swedish summer give generous time on the rock, with light late into the evening. The maritime climate can bring rain, and conditions are best in the warmer, drier spells. The cool air gives good friction, ideal for the slab climbing, and the long summer days, beautiful coastal light, and quality rock combine to make Bohuslän a memorable summer climbing experience.
