Deep in the Karakoram range of northern Pakistan, the Trango Towers rise from the Baltoro Glacier in some of the most spectacular granite walls on Earth. The Great Trango Tower holds one of the largest near-vertical rock faces in the world, and the slender Nameless Tower is among the most striking spires ever climbed. This is high-altitude big-wall climbing at its most extreme, combining the difficulty of granite with the seriousness of a remote, glaciated, high-mountain environment. ## The Setting

The Trango Towers stand above the Baltoro Glacier, on the approach to K2 and the great peaks of the Karakoram. Reaching them requires a long trek through one of the most spectacular mountain landscapes on Earth, surrounded by some of the world's highest peaks. The towers rise from the glacier in immense granite walls and spires, in a setting of overwhelming scale and remoteness. This is one of the great wilderness mountain environments, far from any road or help.

## The Great Trango Tower

The Great Trango Tower is a massive granite peak whose east face is one of the largest near-vertical rock walls on the planet, dropping well over a thousand metres of steep granite. Routes on this face are among the biggest and most serious rock climbs in the world, demanding days of committing big-wall climbing at altitude. The scale of the wall, combined with the remote high-mountain setting, makes it one of the ultimate objectives in big-wall alpinism.

## The Nameless Tower

The Nameless Tower (Trango Tower) is a slender, soaring granite spire, perhaps the most beautiful of the group, rising in a clean pillar to a sharp summit. Its striking profile has made it one of the most celebrated rock peaks in the world, and routes up its steep granite are classics of high-altitude big-wall climbing. The combination of perfect rock, elegant form, and serious altitude makes the Nameless Tower a dream objective for elite climbers.

## High-Altitude Big-Wall Climbing

Climbing the Trango Towers combines the technical difficulty of big-wall granite with the seriousness of high altitude and a remote, glaciated environment. Routes take days, with climbers living on the wall, contending with thin air, cold, and the commitment of being far from any help. This is among the most demanding forms of climbing on Earth, requiring big-wall mastery, alpine experience, and the endurance for a serious expedition.

## Expedition and Commitment

Reaching and climbing the Trango Towers is a full expedition undertaking. The long trek in, the glaciated base camp, the altitude, and the days spent on the walls all demand careful planning and serious commitment. The remoteness means self-sufficiency is essential, and the weather and conditions of the high Karakoram add to the seriousness. For those who take it on, the Trango Towers represent the pinnacle of big-wall ambition in one of the world's greatest mountain ranges.
