On the central coast of Norway, the vast Hanshelleren cave at Flatanger has become one of the most significant venues in modern sport climbing. This enormous overhanging gneiss cave holds some of the hardest routes ever climbed, including pioneering ascents at the very limit of the sport, drawing the world's strongest climbers to a remote and beautiful coastal setting. Flatanger represents the cutting edge of what is possible on rope, in a place of wild Norwegian beauty. Find it on the [map](/map?country=no).

## The Setting

Flatanger lies on the central Norwegian coast, a region of fjords, islands, and rugged coastline. The Hanshelleren cave sits in a hillside above this landscape, a vast natural amphitheatre of overhanging gneiss. The setting is remote and beautiful, far from the major centres, with the sea and the coastal mountains beyond. Reaching this world-class venue means travelling into the wild Norwegian coast, and the combination of the climbing and the setting is part of what makes Flatanger special.

## The Cave

The Hanshelleren cave is an enormous overhanging gneiss formation, one of the steepest and most impressive sport-climbing caves in the world. The rock is gneiss, solid and featured, and the cave's steepness gives long, powerful, endurance-intensive climbing. The scale of the cave and the steepness of the walls create the conditions for routes of extreme difficulty, and it is this combination of quality rock and overhanging terrain that has made Flatanger a venue for the hardest climbing.

## The Hardest Routes

Flatanger holds some of the hardest sport routes ever climbed, including pioneering ascents that pushed the upper limits of the grading scale. These routes, climbed by the world's strongest, represent the cutting edge of sport climbing, long endurance test-pieces up the steep gneiss of the cave. While these extreme lines are the domain of the elite, the cave and surrounding crags also hold powerful, steep climbing across a range of hard grades, drawing strong climbers from around the world.

## Climbing at the Limit

What makes Flatanger remarkable is its place at the very limit of the sport. The routes here have defined what is possible in hard sport climbing, and the cave has hosted the establishment of some of the most significant first ascents in the discipline's history. For most climbers, Flatanger is a place to witness and aspire to the cutting edge, while the strong can test themselves on powerful routes in one of the world's great caves.

## Season and the Remote Coast

Flatanger is a summer destination, shaped by the short, intense Norwegian season and the maritime climate. The long days of the northern summer give time on the rock, while the cave's overhanging nature offers shelter from rain, allowing climbing even in the often-wet coastal weather. The remote setting means climbers come prepared, and the combination of the world-class cave, the wild coast, and the long summer days makes Flatanger a unique and serious destination.
