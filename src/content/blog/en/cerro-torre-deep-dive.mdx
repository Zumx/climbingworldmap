Cerro Torre rises from the Patagonian ice in a slender spire of granite, capped with a mushroom of rime ice, often called one of the most beautiful and difficult mountains on Earth. Battered by the ferocious winds and storms of Patagonia, swept by the great ice cap, it long seemed impossible to climb, and its history is one of the most turbulent and contested in mountaineering. To stand beneath Cerro Torre is to witness one of the world's most extraordinary peaks. Find it on the [map](/map?country=ar).

## The Setting

Cerro Torre stands in the Southern Patagonian Ice Field, near the town of El Chaltén in Argentina, part of a group of slender granite spires that includes the Torre group and, nearby, Fitz Roy. The mountain rises in a sheer, ice-glazed tower, surrounded by glaciers and battered by the relentless Patagonian weather. The setting is one of overwhelming wildness and beauty, where the spires pierce the sky above the ice, and the weather rules everything.

## The Rock and the Ice

Cerro Torre is a granite spire, but it is defined as much by ice as by rock. The upper mountain is plastered with rime ice, the strange feathery accretions formed by the moisture-laden Patagonian winds, and the summit is capped by a famous and fearsome ice mushroom. Climbing the mountain means contending with steep granite, hard ice, and the rime formations, in conditions of extreme severity. It is one of the most demanding combinations of terrain in world climbing.

## The Patagonian Weather

The defining challenge of Cerro Torre is the weather. Patagonia is famous for its ferocious winds and rapidly changing storms, sweeping in off the Southern Ocean and the ice cap. Climbers may wait weeks for a brief weather window, and the mountain's reputation for difficulty is bound up with these brutal conditions. The combination of technical difficulty and savage weather is what long made Cerro Torre seem impossible and what makes any ascent a serious achievement.

## A Turbulent History

Cerro Torre has one of the most contested histories in mountaineering. Disputed claims of early ascents, controversy over a bolt-laden route and its later removal, and decades of debate over what constitutes a legitimate ascent have made the mountain a focus of fierce ethical argument. This turbulent history, alongside the mountain's beauty and difficulty, has given Cerro Torre a unique place in climbing lore, symbolising debates about style, ethics, and the meaning of an ascent.

## Climbing Cerro Torre

Cerro Torre is a serious objective for elite alpinists, demanding technical rock and ice skills, the endurance to wait out the weather, and the boldness to commit when a window opens. The routes are committing and the conditions severe, and an ascent represents a major achievement in modern alpinism. For most, the mountain is something to behold rather than climb — a peak whose beauty and history are part of the great story of Patagonia.
