Stanage Edge runs for nearly six kilometres along a moorland escarpment in England's Peak District, a long line of rough gritstone that is perhaps the most famous and beloved crag in Britain. With more than a thousand routes, from gentle classics to bold and fearsome test-pieces, Stanage is where generations of British climbers have learned their craft and tested their nerve. It is the definitive gritstone edge and a cornerstone of British climbing culture. Find it on the [map](/map?country=gb).

## The Setting

Stanage sits on the moors above the Hope Valley, near Sheffield and Hathersage, a long escarpment of dark gritstone overlooking a landscape of heather and farmland. The edge is easily accessible, popular, and woven into the daily life of the large climbing community in the surrounding cities. On a fine evening, climbers spread along its length, and the combination of accessible rock and beautiful moorland setting is at the heart of its enduring appeal.

## The Rock

Gritstone is a coarse, rough sandstone that gives the climbing at Stanage its distinctive character. The rock is grippy and friction-rich, with rounded breaks, cracks, and slopers rather than positive holds. Gritstone climbing rewards subtle technique, body positioning, and trust in friction, and it has a feel unlike any other rock. The cracks take excellent protection, while the faces and slopers demand boldness and finesse. It is a rock that has shaped a whole tradition of climbing.

## Trad Climbing and the Bold Tradition

Stanage is a trad crag, and gritstone climbing carries a famously bold ethic. The cracks offer good protection, but many face routes and highballs are run-out and committing, demanding a cool head and confidence. This tradition of bold, ground-up trad climbing is central to British climbing identity, and Stanage is where it is most fully expressed. The grades can feel stiff, and the climbing rewards experience and nerve as much as strength.

## Classic Routes

Stanage holds an extraordinary density of classic routes. Lines like Robin Hood's Right Hand, the cracks and aretes along the edge, and famous bold faces have been climbed by generations and remain benchmarks of the grades. The edge offers everything from gentle introductions to gritstone to desperate test-pieces, and the sheer quality and quantity of routes mean a climber can return endlessly and never tire of it. Bouldering along the edge is also superb.

## Season and Conditions

Stanage climbs best in cool, dry conditions, when the gritstone gives its best friction. Spring and autumn are ideal, and crisp winter days can be excellent when dry. Summer can be too warm and humid for the best friction, and gritstone is greasy when damp. The exposed moorland setting means the rock dries quickly after rain but is at the mercy of the British weather. Climbers chase the cool, dry days when friction is at its best.
