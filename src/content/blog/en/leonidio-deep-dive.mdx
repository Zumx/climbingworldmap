On the coast of the Peloponnese in southern Greece, the town of Leonidio sits beneath towering walls of red and orange limestone that have, in little more than a decade, made it one of Europe's premier sport climbing destinations. Thousands of routes, developed largely since the 2010s, climb the dramatic cliffs above the town and the sea, with a mild winter climate that draws climbers when much of Europe is cold. Leonidio is a rising star, and a town transformed by climbing. Find it on the [map](/map?country=gr).

## The Setting

Leonidio lies on the eastern coast of the Peloponnese, beneath striking cliffs of red and orange limestone that rise above the town and the surrounding valleys. The dramatic walls, the warm coastal climate, and the beautiful setting between mountains and sea give Leonidio a spectacular backdrop. The town itself has embraced climbing, with climber-friendly accommodation and a growing infrastructure, and the partnership between climbers and the local community has been central to its rise.

## The Rock

The limestone of Leonidio is superb, varied, and richly featured, ranging in colour from grey to vivid red and orange. The climbing offers everything from technical vertical faces to steep tufa walls and powerful caves, with a wide span of grades and styles. The tufa climbing in particular is a highlight, with the flowing calcite formations giving three-dimensional, sculptural movement. The quality and variety of the rock are a major part of what has driven Leonidio's rapid rise.

## The Rapid Development

Leonidio's transformation has been remarkable. Developed largely since the 2010s by local and international climbers, the area now holds thousands of routes across dozens of sectors, with new development continuing apace. This rapid growth, combined with the quality of the rock and the mild climate, has propelled Leonidio from obscurity to the front rank of European sport climbing in little more than a decade, a striking example of how a destination can be built.

## The Sectors

Leonidio's climbing is spread across many sectors on the cliffs around the town. Areas range from gentle, beginner-friendly walls to steep, hard test-pieces in the caves and on the tufa walls. The spread of grades and styles means the area suits climbers of every level, from those finding their feet to those projecting at their limit. The density and variety mean a climber can spend a long trip exploring without exhausting the options.

## Season and Culture

Leonidio is fundamentally a winter and shoulder-season destination. The mild coastal climate of the southern Peloponnese makes it ideal from autumn through spring, just when northern Europe is cold. Summer is generally too hot. The climbing culture that has grown around the town — the welcoming community, the climber camps, the festivals — is part of the appeal, and the partnership between climbers and locals has made Leonidio a model of how climbing can transform a place.
