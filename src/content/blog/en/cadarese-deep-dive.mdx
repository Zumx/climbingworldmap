In the Val d'Ossola of northern Italy, near the Swiss border, Cadarese is Italy's premier crack-climbing destination, a place where the granite splits into clean, parallel cracks reminiscent of the American desert. It is a rare venue in the Alps — most Alpine granite is climbed on faces and slabs, but Cadarese offers the jamming game, with splitters of every size. For trad climbers and crack enthusiasts, it is a unique and rewarding destination. Find it on the [map](/map?country=it).

## The Setting

Cadarese lies in the Ossola valleys of Piedmont, a region of deep granite valleys running up toward the high Alps and the Swiss border. The crag sits in a beautiful valley setting, surrounded by forest and mountains, with the granite walls offering the cracks that define the area. The setting is peaceful and alpine, and the combination of quality granite and the rare crack-climbing focus has made Cadarese a destination for climbers seeking the jamming game in Europe.

## The Rock and the Cracks

The granite of Cadarese is excellent, splitting into clean, parallel-sided cracks that run up the walls in a way more typical of American desert sandstone than Alpine granite. The cracks come in every size, from fingertips to wide offwidths, demanding the full repertoire of jamming technique. The rock is solid and the protection good, and the quality of the crack lines, on rock of this character, makes Cadarese a special place in the European climbing landscape.

## The Art of Jamming

Cadarese is a place to learn and perfect crack technique. The splitter cracks demand hand jams, finger locks, fist jams, and the dreaded offwidth, and the climbing is a continuous test of jamming skill and efficiency. For climbers raised on face and sport climbing, Cadarese offers a rare European venue to develop the crack skills usually associated with America. The cracks reward technique, efficiency, and the confidence to trust the jam over sustained pitches.

## A Rare Alpine Trad Venue

What makes Cadarese special is its rarity. Most Alpine granite is climbed on faces, slabs, and mixed terrain, and pure crack venues are uncommon in Europe. Cadarese fills this gap, offering the kind of splitter trad climbing that climbers usually travel to America to find. This rarity, combined with the quality of the rock and the beautiful valley setting, gives Cadarese a special place in the European trad scene.

## Season and Conditions

Cadarese climbs best in the warmer months, from spring through autumn, when the granite is dry and the valley is accessible. The Alpine setting means cooler temperatures at altitude, giving good friction, while the lower valley can be pleasant for much of the season. Summer offers the most reliable conditions, and the granite gives its best in cool, dry weather. The valley setting is beautiful through the green months.
