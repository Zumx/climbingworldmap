In the Guangxi region of southern China, Yangshuo sits among one of the most surreal landscapes on Earth — a forest of limestone karst towers rising abruptly from flat rice paddies along the Li River. Over the past decades it has grown into China's premier sport climbing destination, with hundreds of bolted routes on the steep, featured towers. The combination of world-class climbing and the dreamlike karst scenery makes Yangshuo unforgettable. Find it on the [map](/map?country=cn).

## The Setting

Yangshuo lies on the Li River, surrounded by hundreds of karst towers — steep limestone pinnacles that rise like green-clad teeth from the flat valley floor. The landscape, often wrapped in mist, is among the most iconic in China, famous from countless paintings and photographs. Climbing here means ascending these towers directly above rice fields, villages, and the winding river, in a setting of extraordinary natural beauty that draws climbers and travellers alike.

## The Rock

The limestone of Yangshuo's towers is steep, varied, and richly featured. The climbing ranges from technical vertical faces to steep, powerful cave routes draped with tufas, offering a wide span of styles. The rock can be sharp and pocketed, and the steepness of many towers gives pumpy, gymnastic climbing. The variety across the sectors means climbers of every preference and ability find lines to suit them, from gentle routes to hard test-pieces.

## The Sectors

Yangshuo's climbing is spread across many sectors among the towers. Areas like the famous Moon Hill, with its natural arch, the White Mountain, and the many other crags offer hundreds of routes across the grades. Some sectors are gentle and welcoming, ideal for those new to outdoor sport climbing, while others hold steep, hard lines for the strong. The density and variety, set among the karst, make Yangshuo a destination where a climber can explore for weeks.

## The Rise of a Climbing Town

Yangshuo has grown into a true climbing town, with climber-friendly accommodation, gear shops, and a thriving scene that has developed over the past decades. Local and international climbers have established the routes and built an infrastructure that makes Yangshuo accessible and welcoming. The combination of the climbing, the scenery, and the relaxed town atmosphere has made it a magnet for climbers across Asia and beyond.

## Season and Conditions

Yangshuo, in the subtropical south of China, climbs best in the cooler, drier months. Autumn through spring offers the best conditions, with cooler air and good friction, while summer is hot and humid, making climbing uncomfortable. The wet season can bring rain, and climbers chase the drier windows. The cool-season climate, combined with the depth of climbing and the beauty of the karst, makes Yangshuo a fine winter and shoulder-season destination.
