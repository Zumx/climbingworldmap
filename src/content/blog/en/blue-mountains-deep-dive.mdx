West of Sydney, the Blue Mountains rise in a vast plateau of golden sandstone, cut by deep valleys and dramatic escarpments cloaked in eucalyptus forest. Within easy reach of Australia's largest city, the area offers extensive climbing — steep sport routes, classic trad lines, and superb bouldering — on the characteristic sandstone of the region. The combination of accessibility, breadth of climbing, and the beautiful blue-hazed forested setting makes the Blue Mountains a cornerstone of east-coast Australian climbing. Find them on the [map](/map?country=au).

## The Setting

The Blue Mountains are a sandstone plateau and a national park, their name drawn from the blue haze of eucalyptus oil that hangs over the forested valleys. The climbing is concentrated around the towns of Katoomba and Blackheath, where escarpments and cliffs drop into the deep valleys. The setting — sandstone walls above a sea of eucalyptus, with dramatic lookouts and waterfalls — is beautiful, and the easy access from Sydney makes it the everyday climbing ground for the city's large community.

## The Rock

The sandstone of the Blue Mountains is golden, varied, and richly featured, forming steep walls, cracks, and the escarpments that define the climbing. The rock offers everything from steep, pocketed sport routes to classic crack lines and bold faces, and its quality and variety are part of the area's appeal. The sandstone can be soft and is best climbed dry, and the range of angles and features means there is climbing to suit every style and discipline.

## Sport, Trad, and Bouldering

The Blue Mountains hold a full range of climbing. Steep sport routes on the escarpments offer pumpy, powerful climbing, while classic trad lines ascend the cracks and corners. Bouldering is superb, on sandstone blocks scattered through the plateau and valleys. Adventurous multi-pitch outings descend and ascend the escarpments. This breadth means the Blue Mountains reward climbers of every discipline, and the depth of climbing keeps the community returning.

## The Crags and Areas

Climbing in the Blue Mountains is spread across numerous crags around Katoomba, Blackheath, and the surrounding plateau. Areas range from steep sport venues to classic trad cliffs and bouldering fields, each with its own character. The density of crags within easy reach, combined with the spectacular setting, means a climber can explore for a long time, following the conditions and the style they prefer through the seasons.

## Season and Conditions

The Blue Mountains climb well through much of the year. The temperate climate offers pleasant conditions in the cooler months, while summer can be warm but is climbable, especially in shade. The sandstone is best climbed dry, and the area can be damp after rain, so climbers chase the dry windows. The accessibility from Sydney means climbers can take advantage of good conditions easily, and the spread of aspects allows climbing year-round.
