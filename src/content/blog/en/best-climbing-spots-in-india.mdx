India is a subcontinent of staggering scale, and its climbing reflects that range, from sun-baked granite boulders in the south to soaring Himalayan walls in the north. Among the ancient ruins of Hampi, climbers traverse warm grey granite above paddy fields, while the orange cliffs of Badami hold some of the country's finest sport routes. Higher up, the Himalaya offer expeditions among the greatest mountains on Earth. The areas below are the essentials; find them all on the [map](/map?country=in).

## Hampi

In Karnataka, the boulder fields around Hampi are India's most famous climbing destination, set amid a UNESCO World Heritage landscape of temples and ruins. Thousands of granite boulders, weathered into rounded forms, scatter across the hills and the banks of the Tungabhadra river. The friction is superb, the lines range from gentle slabs to powerful overhangs, and the backdrop of ancient stone is unlike anywhere else. The Goan Corner guesthouse has long been the hub of the bouldering scene, and the cool winter months draw climbers from around the world.

## Badami

Also in Karnataka, Badami is India's premier sport climbing crag, its horseshoe of red sandstone cliffs rising above an ancient town and reservoir. The rock holds hundreds of bolted routes, from gentle warm-ups to steep, pumpy test pieces, on featured sandstone that has drawn international attention. The setting, with its cave temples carved into the cliffs and monkeys patrolling the walls, is unforgettable. Badami climbs best in the cooler, dry winter, and pairs naturally with a bouldering trip to nearby Hampi.

## The Himalaya

The Indian Himalaya hold some of the most spectacular mountaineering on the planet. The states of Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Ladakh and Sikkim offer peaks from accessible trekking summits to serious technical objectives. The Gangotri region, with granite spires such as Shivling and the Bhagirathi peaks, is a famous arena for big alpine and big-wall routes. Ladakh's high desert ranges and the granite of the Miyar valley add further scope. Expeditions here demand acclimatisation, permits and respect for altitude, weather and remoteness.

## Granite Domes of the South

Beyond Hampi and Badami, southern India holds extensive granite that remains lightly developed. The hills around Bangalore offer bouldering and short routes within reach of the city, including the well-known Ramanagara domes used as a film location and now a climbing area. Tamil Nadu and Andhra Pradesh hold further granite outcrops and domes. For the explorer, the south rewards those willing to seek out crags, with warm rock and a long climbing season outside the monsoon.

## The Urban Gyms

India's climbing community has grown rapidly, and modern gyms now anchor the scene in the major cities. Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai and other hubs hold bouldering and lead walls that train a new generation of climbers. Equilibrium Climbing Station in Mumbai, Delhi Rock and others have helped build a competition and youth scene, while university walls and clubs feed climbers out to Hampi, Badami and the mountains. Indoor climbing has become the entry point for a fast-growing outdoor community.

## Season and Travel

India's climate dictates the climbing calendar. The southern crags of Hampi and Badami climb best from late autumn through early spring, when temperatures are cooler and the monsoon has passed; summer brings fierce heat. The Himalaya, by contrast, open in the warmer months, with the main expedition seasons in late spring and after the monsoon in autumn. Travel can be slow and chaotic, but the rewards, from ancient ruins to the highest mountains on Earth, are extraordinary.
