El Capitan rises a thousand metres above the floor of Yosemite Valley, a single sweep of pale granite that is among the most famous walls on earth. Of the many routes that thread its enormous face, none is more celebrated than Freerider. It is the line that brought big-wall free climbing to a global audience, the route Alex Honnold soloed without a rope in 2017, a feat documented in the film that won an Academy Award. Yet long before that morning, Freerider was already the most coveted free climb on the wall, the proving ground for a generation of Yosemite climbers.

## El Capitan and the Granite of Yosemite

El Capitan is carved from Yosemite's glacier-polished granite, a rock of immense scale and subtle features. The wall is so vast that climbers spend days on it, sleeping on ledges or hanging portaledges. The granite offers cracks of every size, smooth faces, and the polished glassiness that makes Yosemite climbing so demanding. The valley itself, with its waterfalls, the Merced River, and the looming presence of Half Dome across the way, is a place of overwhelming grandeur, and El Capitan is its centrepiece, drawing climbers from around the world.

## The Birth of Freerider

Freerider was established by the German brothers Alexander and Thomas Huber around the turn of the millennium, building on the earlier Salathé Wall, the route from which it largely derives. Where the Salathé followed a thin and ferociously hard headwall, Freerider found a slightly easier variation, bringing the free ascent of El Capitan within reach of more climbers. It quickly became the benchmark: a roughly thirty-pitch free route up the full height of the wall, graded around 5.13a, that demanded mastery of every Yosemite skill.

## The Crux Pitches

Freerider is defined by a handful of brutal pitches strung between long stretches of sustained climbing. The Monster Offwidth is a body-swallowing crack that must be fought with the whole body. The Boulder Problem, high on the wall, is the technical crux, a sequence of tiny holds where a single subtle foot placement decides success or failure. The Enduro Corner is a long, pumping dihedral that tests the forearms after days of effort. Each crux sits within a route that is already a major undertaking, and stringing them together free, in a single push, is the great challenge.

## Honnold's Free Solo

On the morning of June 3, 2017, Alex Honnold climbed Freerider with no rope and no protection, the first person ever to free solo El Capitan. The ascent took just under four hours and is widely regarded as one of the greatest athletic achievements in any sport. The Boulder Problem, with its karate-kick move to a distant hold, became the most scrutinised sequence in climbing. The film Free Solo brought the route, and the wall, to millions who had never heard of Yosemite, and turned Freerider into a household name far beyond the climbing world.

## Climbing It Roped

For the vast majority of climbers, Freerider is a roped objective of enormous difficulty and ambition. A free ascent typically takes several days, with the team hauling supplies and sleeping on ledges like the famous El Cap Spire or the Block. Even climbers who do not free every pitch aspire to climb the route, often aiding through the hardest sections. The logistics — hauling water, managing the wall, enduring the heat and exposure — are as demanding as the climbing itself, and a successful ascent is a milestone in any climber's life.

## Season, Permits, and the Valley

El Capitan is best climbed in spring and autumn, when temperatures are moderate; summer brings fierce heat on the sun-baked wall, and winter is cold and short on daylight. Yosemite National Park manages the valley closely, and climbers must follow regulations on bivouacs, waste, and parking. The valley fills with visitors in peak season, yet the wall above remains a world apart, a vertical realm where climbers live for days. Camp 4, the historic climbers' campground, remains the cultural heart of Yosemite climbing.

## The Legacy of Freerider

Freerider stands as a symbol of what big-wall free climbing can be. It bridged the gap between the aid-climbing pioneers who first conquered El Capitan and the free climbers who now dance up its face, and it became, through Honnold's solo, the most famous rock climb in the world. For Yosemite regulars it remains a lifetime goal, a route that demands years of preparation. For the wider public it is the line that revealed, more vividly than any other, what climbers are capable of on the great granite walls.
